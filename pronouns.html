My Pronouns

Thank you for being interested in my pronouns, I appreciate it! I will explain the most important things here on this page but will also provide resources for more information.

What pronouns do I use?

Primarily I use they/them in english and dey/deren in german.
I am also comfortable with most pronouns (think neopronouns), as long as you don't use it/its or any equivalent of it in other languages. I would really appreciate it, if you could switch my pronouns up a bit and don't just stick to one.

They/Them

Subjectiv Objective Possesive Reflexiv
they them their themself

They/Them are pronouns that are commonly known as plural, but can be used in a singular way, if a person prfers it or if you don't know a persons gender (imagine you find an umbrella: "Oh, somebody has lost their umbrella"). So you probably use it all the time without knowing and this type of usage is recorded as early as the 14th century, only about one century after plural they emerged and around the same time singular you appeared.
They/Them is also widely used as a pronoun for nonbinary* (if you are not familiar with the *, its the german gender star and mostly used between the maskulin and feminin form to include everyone else, but can be used like here, to include not only people that use the label nonbinary but also people that might fall under the nonbinary umbrella) people and is often considered the most well known gender neutral alternative to he and she and more humane than it (though only use they/them if prople asked you so and respect it if people use neopronouns or it!).

Dey/Deren

Nominativ Genitiv Dativ Akkusativ
dey deren denen dey

Dey/Deren are german neopronouns that are designed to be similar to the english they/them. Dey mimics they in its pronounciation (the spelling is fit to the german language) and deren/denen are traditionally plural but used in a singular way, kind of mimicing the way, they can be singular or plural.
